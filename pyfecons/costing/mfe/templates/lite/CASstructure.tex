\section{Cost Accounting Structure}

\textbf{Description:} The Cost Accounting Structure (CAS) table provides a hierarchical breakdown of all capital costs associated with the fusion power plant. The structure follows the GENIV Economics Modeling Working Group (G4EMWG) methodology, organizing costs into major categories: Pre-construction costs (Category 10), Direct Costs (Category 20) including structures, reactor plant equipment, turbine plant equipment, and other direct capital costs, and Indirect Costs (Categories 30-60) including capitalized indirect service costs, owner's costs, supplementary costs, and financial costs. Each major category is further subdivided into specific cost accounts (e.g., Category 22.1.1 for First Wall \& Blanket), allowing for detailed tracking of cost components. The table shows both absolute costs in millions of USD and percentages relative to Total Capital Cost (TCC), enabling comparison with historical fusion plant designs such as ARIES-AT. This structure is fundamental for understanding the cost drivers and cost distribution across different plant systems.
